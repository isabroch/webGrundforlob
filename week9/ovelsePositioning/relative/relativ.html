Broken Social Scene: "Broken Social Scene"

After a year of recording and writing, Broken Social Scene is a fine distillation of all the band's ambitions. Hands-in-the-air anthems, daydream comedowns, sprawling jams -- this is a long, winding road of a record that goes places to which even BSS are surprised to have arrived.

Stars: "In Our Bedroom After The War"

Acclaimed Canadian quintet, Stars, are touring Canada alongside the release of a new release which features all new songs, written and recorded this past summer in the bands hometown of Montreal. Stars will unveil the beauty and tragedy of Sad Robots with an all new live show, where they perform songs from all four full-lengths, including In Our Bedroom After The War.

The Most Serene Republic: "Population"

The album was self-produced by the band in Toronto over the last year, and was inspired by the sprawling suburbs of their hometown of Milton, Ontario. Musically, they sound as grandiose as ever. On Population, they showcase their prog and jazz influence, while building on the unique sound they carved out with their Arts   Crafts debut, Underwater Cinematographer.

Los Campesinos!: "We Are Beautiful, We Are Doomed"

The youthful septet from Cardiff, Wales, Los Campesinos! prove they have no shortage of ingenuity, creativity, and most importantly, energy, with the announcement of their new record.

Constantines: "Hard Feelings, Easy Money"

Constantines' music, as well as their thundering and inspired live shows, have continually earned the band a loyal following from both music writers and music fans alike, drawing comparisons to The Clash, Bruce Springsteen, Fugazi and Nick Cave.
